## Checking Out Books

### In-Person Checkout
1. Find the item you want to borrow
2. Note the **call number** or bring to circulation desk
3. Present your **library card** to staff
4. Staff will check out the item to your account
5. Note the **due date** on your receipt
6. Take the item home

### Maximum Borrowing Limits
- **Default limit**: 5 items at a time
- **Loan period**: Typically 14-21 days (varies by item type)
- **Renewals**: Usually allowed if no holds exist

### What You Cannot Check Out
- Items marked as "Reference Only"
- Items already on hold for someone else
- Items marked as "In Processing"
- Items restricted to in-library use

## Placing Holds

### What is a Hold?
A hold (or reservation) lets you reserve an item that is currently checked out. You'll be notified when it becomes available for pickup.

### How to Place a Hold
1. Search for the desired publication
2. Click on the title to view details
3. Click **Place Hold** button
4. Confirm your request
5. You'll receive a confirmation message

### Hold Status
- **Waiting**: You're in line waiting for the item
- **Ready for Pickup**: Item is available at circulation desk
- **Expired**: You didn't pick up in time (hold canceled)

### Picking Up Holds
1. Wait for **notification** (email or text)
2. Visit the library within the pickup period (usually 7 days)
3. Go to circulation desk
4. Present your library card
5. Staff will check out the item to you

### Canceling a Hold
1. Go to **My Account**
2. Find the hold under **Active Holds**
3. Click **Cancel Hold**
4. Confirm cancellation

## Renewing Items

### How to Renew
1. Log into your account
2. Go to **My Account**
3. Under **Active Loans**, find the item
4. Click **Renew** button next to the item
5. New due date will be displayed

### Renewal Limits
- **Maximum renewals**: Usually 2-3 times per item
- **Restrictions**: Cannot renew if:
  - Item has holds from other users
  - Item is already overdue
  - You've reached renewal limit
  - Your account is blocked

### Renewal Tips
- ‚úÖ Renew before the due date
- ‚úÖ Renew early if you still need the item
- ‚úÖ Check if renewal was successful
- ‚ùå Don't wait until the last day

## Returning Items

### How to Return
1. **In Person**: Return to circulation desk during library hours
2. **Book Drop**: Use 24-hour book drop (if available)
3. **Other Locations**: Some branches accept returns

### When to Return
- By the **due date** shown on your receipt
- Check **My Account** for due dates
- Set reminders on your phone/calendar

### What to Return
- The complete item (all parts)
- Any supplementary materials (CDs, DVDs, etc.)
- In good condition

### Late Returns
- Items returned after due date are considered **overdue**
- You may face consequences:
  - Overdue notices
  - Account suspension
  - Inability to borrow more items
- Return items as soon as possible

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Account & Access

**Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?**  
A: Click "Forgot Password" on the login page and follow instructions sent to your email.

**Q: Can I change my email address?**  
A: Yes, go to My Account > Edit Profile and update your email.

**Q: Why is my account blocked?**  
A: Common reasons include overdue items, excessive fines, or policy violations. Contact library staff.

### Borrowing & Returns

**Q: How many items can I borrow?**  
A: The default limit is 5 items, but this may vary based on your account type.

**Q: Can I return items early?**  
A: Yes, you can return items anytime before the due date.

**Q: What happens if I lose an item?**  
A: Report it immediately to library staff. You may be charged a replacement fee.

**Q: Can someone else return my items?**  
A: Yes, items can be returned by anyone at the circulation desk or book drop.

### Holds & Renewals

**Q: How long do I have to pick up a hold?**  
A: Usually 7 days from notification. Check with your library for specific policies.

**Q: Can I renew an overdue item?**  
A: No, return overdue items first before borrowing or renewing others.

**Q: How many times can I renew?**  
A: Typically 2-3 times, unless there are holds or restrictions.

**Q: Can I place a hold on an available item?**  
A: Usually no; available items should be borrowed directly.

### Searching & Finding

**Q: How do I find items on a specific topic?**  
A: Use the Advanced Search with subject filters, or browse by subject category.

**Q: What does the call number mean?**  
A: It's like the item's address in the library, helping you locate it on shelves.

**Q: Can I suggest items for the library to acquire?**  
A: Contact library staff with your suggestions.

### Technical Issues

**Q: The website isn't working. What should I do?**  
A: Try refreshing, clearing cache, or using a different browser. Contact IT support if problems persist.

**Q: Can I access the library from my phone?**  
A: Yes, the website is mobile-friendly and works on smartphones and tablets.

**Q: Is there an app for the e-Library?**  
A: Currently, access is through the web browser. An app may be available in the future.
